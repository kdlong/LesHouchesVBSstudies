\subsubsection*{Parton level comparisons}

We start the comparison of the various predictions by a comparison at the level of the cross section.
The fiducial volume is the one described in Eqs.~\eqref{cut:1}-\eqref{cut:4}.
The results are documented in Tables~\ref{table:xsectLOfix} and \ref{table:xsectLOdyn}.
The cross sections for both processes ${\rm p} {\rm p} \to {\rm e}^+  \nu_{\rm e}  \mu^+ \mu^- {\rm j} {\rm j}$ and ${\rm p} {\rm p} \to {\rm e}^-  \bar \nu_{\rm e}  \mu^+ \mu^- {\rm j} {\rm j}$ at fixed and dynamical scales are presented.
The predictions are clearly not in statistical agreement but they usually agree within about one per cent apart from {\sc MG5\_aMC}.
For the later, this is probably due to a miss configuration of the run.
In general, too aggressive estimations of the statistical errors as well as miss configurations of the run cannot be excluded.

As it can be see in Fig.~\ref{vbs_fig_fixed_order}, the agreements between the various predictions is usually rather good in both shape and normalisation.
There, the invariant mass and rapidity separation of the two tagging jets are displayed.
The larger discrepancy is found in the region of low rapidity-separation between the two jets.
The difference is the largest between {\sc MoCaNLO}+{\sc Recola} and {\sc VBFNLO}.
This can be explained by the different matrix element used.
Indeed, {\sc VBFNLO} does not use the full matrix element and such differences (even is not that large) have been already observed for ${\rm W}^\pm{\rm W}^\pm{\rm j}{\rm j}$ \cite{Anders:2018gfr}.
Nonetheless, apart from this first bin, {\sc VBFNLO} seems to approximate rather well the full computations in the fiducial region chosen.
Therefore, as for the ${\rm W}^\pm{\rm W}^\pm{\rm j}{\rm j}$ \cite{Anders:2018gfr}, the VBS approximation seems to be below the current experimental accuracy.
